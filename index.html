Trump's been talking about Greenland. Why?

Greenland is a large island that sits between Canada, Europe and Russia. It's part of the Arctic Circle, which is why most of the island is covered by a massive sheet of ice. Greenland also has natural resources like oil, gas and critical minerals that are mostly untapped. Taking over Greenland is important for national security, according to Trump. The island is in a key strategic position for the U.S. because it can provide early defence for potential attacks coming in from Russia. It's a good place for radar, military bases and missile defence systems, and Trump has said he needs Greenland because Russia and China want to take it over.

Ontario had an earthquake. Did you know Canada has 4,000 a year?

Thousands of Ontarians felt the ground shake beneath their feet this week. Just before 11 p.m. ET on Jan. 27, Earthquakes Canada recorded a magnitude 3.7 quake in central and Southern Ontario. It was felt from Kingston all the way to Kitchener, according to Natural Resources Canada. So far there haven't been any reports of injuries or damage. A 3.7-magnitude earthquake is considered weak, and it's very unlikely for those under a magnitude of five to cause damage.

Careful! Many Pizza Pops products currently unsafe to eat

This week, the Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) added several more flavours of Pizza Pops to a previous recall that began on Dec. 21, 2025.

The recall is due to the possible contamination from a dangerous bacteria called E. coli that can make you sick. Last month, several people were hospitalized after the first recall.

Which city got the most snowfall? And where does it all go?

Were you one of the thousands of Canadians enjoying the snowfall this week? Whether you love it or loathe it, much of the country got lots of snow.

John Pomeroy, a professor of geography studies how the snow affects the environment. Snow recharges soil with moisture and often replenishes nearby lakes, rivers and streams. That process is great for our water systems, said Pomeroy.

You've been waiting all year, and now it's finally time to give a big shoutout to one of the most commonly seen creatures across our country. Why many Canadians saw dazzling northern lights this week

An impressive solar storm could be seen from parts of Northern Canada this week. Solar storms are caused by flares of energy travelling from the sun's surface into space. When this energy makes contact with Earth, it creates a phenomenon known as the northern lights. The scientific name is aurora borealis.
